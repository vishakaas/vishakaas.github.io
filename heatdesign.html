Heat Exchanger Design

Objective: Research and design a heat exchanger that is viable to operate in a high volume chemical plant with a production rate of 20,000 to 200,000 tonnes/year.

I designed a 2-8 shell-and-tube heat exchanger using an iterative approach on Microsoft Excel to determine the unknown dimensions with the provided operating conditions and heuristics. The dimensions were varied based on a trial and error procedure, so that the calculated overall heat transfer coefficient converges within ± 5% of the initial estimate.

I received 94% on the individual portion of the project.

Following the individual design configuration, I worked in a team of 4 to propose the optimal configuration in a formal report based on the attempt to minimize heat surface area (capital cost), while maintaining a suitable pressure drop (operating cost).

The report covered the following: executive summary, background information, design method & analysis, TEMA specification sheet for the final configuration, and sample calculations.
