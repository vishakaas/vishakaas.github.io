Accessibility on Demand

Objective: Design a learning space on campus that accommodates up to 100 learners with diverse abilities.

I worked in a group of 6 to represent Accessibility on Demand, a design firm, that advocates and incorporates vital accessible methods into new buildings to support the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (2005) and drive Ontario to a barrier-free zone.

The user/design, ethical, political, cultural, and business impacts along with the final work plan were thoroughly researched to reach the final design.

Our final RFP was selected to be incorporated into an ongoing accessible classroom plan within Ryerson's Centre for Excellence in Teaching and Learning.
